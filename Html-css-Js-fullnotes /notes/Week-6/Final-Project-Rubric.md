# Final Project Rubric  
## Grading Categories and Criteria

Use this rubric to assess the **final multi-page website** (portfolio or small business style).  
Adjust score boundaries to match your program (e.g. pass/fail vs letter grade).

---

## Overall

| Category              | Weight | Purpose |
|-----------------------|--------|--------|
| HTML structure        | 20%    | Semantic markup, accessibility, validity. |
| CSS layout & responsiveness | 25% | Layout, Flexbox, media queries, polish. |
| JavaScript logic      | 25%    | Correctness, DOM, events, optional persistence/API. |
| Code cleanliness      | 15%    | Organization, naming, comments, no dead code. |
| Creativity / polish   | 15%    | Design, UX, completeness, risk-taking. |

**Total: 100%**

---

## 1. HTML structure (20%)

| Criteria | Excellent (18–20) | Good (14–17) | Needs work (10–13) | Poor (0–9) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Semantic tags** | Consistent use of `header`, `nav`, `main`, `section`, `article`, `footer` where appropriate. | Most pages use semantic structure; a few `div`-only sections. | Some semantic use; many generic `div`s. | Little or no semantic structure. |
| **Document structure** | Valid DOCTYPE, `html`, `head`, `body`; one logical `h1` per page; sensible heading hierarchy. | Minor issues (e.g. two `h1`s on one page or skipped levels). | Several structure issues. | Broken or invalid structure. |
| **Accessibility & best practices** | All images have meaningful `alt`; links are descriptive; form labels present. | One or two missing `alt`s or labels. | Multiple missing `alt`s or unclear links. | No attention to alt text or labels. |
| **Multi-page & navigation** | At least 4 pages; same nav on each; all links work and paths correct. | 4 pages; nav mostly consistent; 1–2 broken links. | Fewer than 4 pages or several broken links. | Missing pages or navigation broken. |

---

## 2. CSS layout & responsiveness (25%)

| Criteria | Excellent (23–25) | Good (18–22) | Needs work (12–17) | Poor (0–11) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Layout (Flexbox/Grid)** | Clear use of Flexbox (or Grid) for nav, cards, sections; layout is intentional. | Flexbox used; one or two layout quirks. | Flexbox used minimally or incorrectly. | No real layout system; floats or ad-hoc positioning. |
| **Responsive design** | Mobile-first or consistent breakpoints; readable and usable on mobile, tablet, desktop. | Works on different sizes; minor overflow or spacing issues. | Only works well on one screen size. | Not responsive or very broken on mobile. |
| **Consistency** | One coherent theme (colors, fonts, spacing) across all pages. | Mostly consistent; small inconsistencies. | Noticeable inconsistency. | No clear theme or very inconsistent. |
| **Polish** | Thoughtful spacing, hover states, focus states; no major visual bugs. | Some polish; a few rough edges. | Basic styling only; little polish. | Unstyled or broken styling. |

---

## 3. JavaScript logic (25%)

| Criteria | Excellent (23–25) | Good (18–22) | Needs work (12–17) | Poor (0–11) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Features** | At least two solid features (e.g. nav toggle, theme switch, form validation, dynamic list, localStorage). | Two features; one may be simple or partly broken. | One feature works; second missing or broken. | No working interactive features. |
| **Correctness** | Logic is correct; edge cases (empty input, missing element) handled; no console errors in normal use. | One or two small bugs or console warnings. | Several bugs or console errors. | Features don't work or many errors. |
| **DOM & events** | Appropriate use of selectors, event listeners, and DOM updates; script runs after DOM ready. | Minor issues (e.g. one wrong selector or missing null check). | Repeated issues with DOM or events. | Script doesn't run or doesn't update the page. |
| **Optional (bonus)** | Extra: localStorage, simple API fetch, or third feature. | — | — | — |

---

## 4. Code cleanliness (15%)

| Criteria | Excellent (14–15) | Good (11–13) | Needs work (8–10) | Poor (0–7) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Organization** | Clear folders (e.g. `css/`, `js/`, `images/`); separate files for HTML, CSS, JS. | Mostly organized; one or two misplaced files. | Some organization; mixed or messy structure. | No clear structure. |
| **Naming** | Descriptive, consistent names (files, classes, IDs, variables). | Mostly good names; a few vague or inconsistent. | Many vague or inconsistent names. | Random or misleading names. |
| **Comments** | Key sections or non-obvious logic briefly commented. | A few comments where helpful. | Rare or unhelpful comments. | No comments or misleading ones. |
| **Dead code / errors** | No commented-out blocks left in; no console errors in normal use. | One or two minor leftovers or warnings. | Noticeable dead code or repeated errors. | Lots of dead code or errors. |

---

## 5. Creativity / polish (15%)

| Criteria | Excellent (14–15) | Good (11–13) | Needs work (8–10) | Poor (0–7) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Design** | Cohesive, readable design; appropriate typography and spacing. | Pleasant and mostly coherent. | Generic or rough. | No real design or hard to read. |
| **Completeness** | All required pages and features done; nothing obviously missing. | One small thing missing or incomplete. | Several incomplete parts. | Project incomplete. |
| **Extra effort** | Clear extra (e.g. extra page, API, animation, or thoughtful UX). | One clear extra touch. | Minimal extra. | No extra effort. |

---

## How to use this rubric

1. **Share the rubric** with students at the start of the final project so they know how they'll be assessed.
2. **Score each category** using the bands above (or convert to points, e.g. Excellent = full category points).
3. **Add short comments** per category (e.g. "Strong Flexbox use; add one more breakpoint for large screens").
4. **Optional:** Use the same categories for peer or self-assessment before submission.

---

## Suggested grade bands (adjust to your program)

- **A (90–100):** Excellent in most categories; only minor gaps.
- **B (80–89):** Good across the board; a few areas "Needs work."
- **C (70–79):** Meets minimums; several "Needs work" or one "Poor."
- **D (60–69):** Multiple "Needs work" or "Poor" in one important category.
- **F (0–59):** Critical gaps (e.g. no responsiveness, no JS features, or project not submitted).

---

## Reflection (soft skill — not graded, but required)

Ask each student to submit short answers (e.g. 2–3 sentences each):

1. **What was the hardest part of this project?**
2. **What's one bug you fixed or one thing you debugged?**
3. **What would you improve or add if you had more time?**

Use these in the final session for discussion and to reinforce that reflection is part of being a developer. This supports the "soft skill element" of the curriculum without adding a numeric score.
